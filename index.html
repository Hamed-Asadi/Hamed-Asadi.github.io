About

I am an MSc-trained environmental researcher with a background in GIS-based spatial modelling, environmental planning, and biodiversity-related analysis. My research has developed from structured spatial decision-support and suitability mapping toward climate-biodiversity modelling using R-based analytical workflows.

My MSc-derived research led to a 2022 GeoJournal publication on a hybrid GIS-OWA and DANP framework for identifying and evaluating ecotourism attractions, where I served as corresponding author. More recently, I have contributed to collaborative ecological research involving R-based analysis and climate-related biodiversity assessment.

Alongside my academic work, I have more than a decade of professional experience as an environmental consultant in environmental and geotechnical laboratory services, supporting industrial and urban development projects through environmental impact assessment, pollution analysis, and practical waste/wastewater management solutions.

I am currently seeking funded PhD opportunities, with a particular interest in New Zealand and Australia, in research areas related to spatial environmental modelling, climate change impacts on biodiversity, conservation planning, and applied data-driven environmental research.

Current direction: integrating GIS expertise with growing R-based modelling skills for climate-informed biodiversity and conservation research.
